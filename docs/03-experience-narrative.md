## 3. The Experience Page (/experience)
*A story, not a resume. Told chronologically so the arc makes sense: engineer who learned systems deeply, got the business layer at ISB, moved into enterprise product, then startup chaos, then running 25 startups at once, then building solo. Each role is one clear idea, not a data dump.*

### National Instruments — Staff Software Engineer (2016-2019)
"I started by writing code. Three years of building signal processing algorithms for 4G and 5G test and measurement systems. Customers like Qualcomm and Verizon used what I built to validate their chipsets. It taught me how complex systems actually work under the surface, which turned out to be the most useful thing I could have learned before moving into product."

### ISB — MBA (2019-2020)
"Indian School of Business. Global #12 MBA per Financial Times. After three years of deep engineering, I wanted the business and strategy layer. This is where I made the shift from engineer to product."

### HCL Technologies — Product Manager (2020-2021)
"My first product role. I owned a B2B SaaS platform that managed 9M+ network devices across 10+ countries for AT&T, Vodafone, and Shaw. Enterprise scale, long sales cycles, the kind of environment where you learn to think in systems and stakeholders. It gave me the foundation for everything that came after."

### Bulls and Apes Project — Head of Product (2022-2023)
"Then the opposite of enterprise. I joined a US-based blockchain gaming startup as founding team and built the product division from nothing. A 20+ person cross-functional team, a gaming ecosystem built around 23,000+ digital assets, and Ethereum's first customizable 3D avatar platform. The whole thing did $8M in cumulative revenue. This is where I learned what 0-to-1 actually feels like when there's no playbook and you're making it up as you go."

### KEY Difference — Head of Product (2024-2026)
"After doing 0-to-1 for one startup, I did it for 25 at the same time. Dubai-based Web3 accelerator and venture studio. I built and ran the entire go-to-market program from scratch: how we select startups, how we support founders, how the accelerator operates. Portfolio startups reached 100K+ users and $10M+ in platform transactions.

Running strategy for that many startups in parallel is where I also noticed an AI capability gap in the org. So I built one myself: an autonomous news aggregator, shipped to the Play Store. That was the start of the solo building."
