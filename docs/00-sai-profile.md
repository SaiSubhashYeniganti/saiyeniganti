## Voice & Content Rules

These rules govern ALL content on this site. Any AI editing copy must follow them.

### Tone
- **Peer energy, not petitioner energy.** Sai is presenting work, not asking for approval.
- **Direct, not promotional.** Say what was built. Don't dress it up.
- **Human, not corporate.** "I built it." not "Leveraging AI-first workflows, I architected an autonomous ingestion pipeline."
- **Proof-first.** Lead with numbers and facts. "86 pages, 65,000 words" before explaining what it is.

### Specific Rules
1. **No em dashes.** Sai doesn't like them. Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence.
2. **No invented experience.** Every claim must be traceable to the career arc above. Creative framing is fine, fabrication is not.
3. **No Awwwards claims.** Don't call any project "Awwwards-level" or similar. Let the work speak.
4. **No filler phrases.** No "leveraging," "architecting," "orchestrating," "autonomous ingestion pipeline," "production-grade systems." Say it simply.
5. **No checklist descriptions.** "Brand, UX, copy, code, deployment" as a list lacks weight. Weave it into a sentence or describe what you actually did.
6. **Max two proof points per paragraph.** Attention dies after that.
7. **NI framing is critical.** Sai built test and measurement solutions AT National Instruments. Qualcomm and Verizon were CUSTOMERS who used those solutions. He did not write code for them.

### Reference Style (from Sai's actual LinkedIn messages)
This is the gold standard for how Sai describes his work:

> "86 pages, 65,000 words of content. Experiment in design and content at scale. One of the best dermatology websites in India."

> "Experiment in proving AI-driven design can be intentional and world-class. Client call to production in a month."

> "Experiment in automation. Web3 news app, shipped to Play Store."

> "Challenge of building a company solo. App, website, brand, infrastructure."

> "Client work. Author's website. Shipped."

Notice: short, factual, no fluff, no selling. Each description is one or two sentences max. The work does the talking.
